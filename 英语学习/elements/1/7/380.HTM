noun countable usually singular 
PLACE 位置 
 ESSENTIAL  
■the part of a building, object or person's body which faces forward or which is most often seen or used 前面；正面
•The front of the museum is very impressive.博物馆的正面极为壮观。
•He spilt soup all down his front.他把汤洒得前襟上都是。
•He was lying on his front.他俯卧着。
•The shop front occupies a very prominent position on the main street.这家商店的门脸在大街上占据了一个非常显眼的位置。
 ESSENTIAL  
■the part of a vehicle which is nearest to its direction of movement （车辆的）前部
•Do you want to sit in the front (= next to the driver) ?你想坐到前座上来吗？
•If we sit near the front of the bus, we'll have a better view.如果我们坐在公车的前部，视野就会好一些。
 ESSENTIAL  
■the outside part or cover of a book, newspaper, or magazine （书刊等的）封面；（报纸的）头版
•There was a picture of the Trevi fountain on the front of the book.书的封面上有一张特里威喷泉的图片。
 ESSENTIAL  
■one of the first pages in a book （书的）前几页中的一页
•There's an inscription in the front of the book.书的前面一页上有题词。
in front 
 ESSENTIAL  
■further forward than someone or something else 在前面
•The car in front suddenly stopped and I went into the back of it.前面的车突然停住，结果我就撞上车尾了。
•She started talking to the man in front of her.她开始跟前面的那个男人攀谈起来。
 IMPROVER  
■winning a game or competition （在比赛或竞赛中）取胜，领先
•By half time the Italians were well in front.半场时义大利队遥遥领先。
in front of 
 ESSENTIAL  
■close to the front part of something 在…之前
•There's parking space in front of the hotel.旅馆前有停车的地方。
 IMPROVER  
■where someone can see or hear you 当着（某人）的面，在（某人）面前
•Please don't swear in front of the children.当着孩子们的面请不要骂人。
up front 
■If you give someone an amount of money up front, you pay them before they do something for you 预付；提前支付
•He wants all the money up front or he won't do the job.他要求预付全款，否则他就不接这工作。
